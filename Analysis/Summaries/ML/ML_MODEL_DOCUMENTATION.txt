1. DATA PRE-PROCESSING & NORMALIZATION
--------------------------------------
Before any modeling was performed, the raw NCRB incident counts were transformed into "Crime Intensity Rates" to ensure statistical fairness across states of varying sizes.

- Normalization: Population-based scaling using 2021 census projections.
- Primary Metric: Intensity Index = (Crime Count / Population in Lakhs).
- Feature Engineering: Multi-year trend smoothing and wide-form pivoting to create a "State Profile" for each domain.
- Missing Data: Imputed using zero-fill (assuming non-reporting in certain IPC/SLL sections).

2. MACHINE LEARNING MODELS
--------------------------

[MODEL A: DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise)]
- Purpose: State Archetype Discovery.
- Goal: To automatically group states that have similar "Crime Signatures" without human bias.
- Hyperparameters: eps=2.0, min_samples=2.
- Evaluation Metric: Silhouette Score & Cluster Distribution.
- Key Finding: Identified India as having low geographic clustering; most states exist as "Noise/Outliers," meaning crime profiles are highly individualized to specific states.

[MODEL B: XGBOOST REGRESSOR (Extreme Gradient Boosting)]
- Purpose: High-Fidelity Trend Forecasting (The "Intensity" Metric).
- Goal: To predict the Intensity Index for the next year (2026) based on 5-year growth coefficients.
- Target Variable: Total Normalized Domain Intensity.
- Evaluation Metric: R-Squared (R2) Accuracy.
- Accuracy Results:
    * Women Domain: 87.26% (Excellent Fit)
    * Human Trafficking: 58.40% (Moderate Fit)
    * Juvenile Domain: 11.34% (Emerging Trend)
- Insights: This model powers the "Predicted Intensity Index" in the dashboard.

[MODEL C: RANDOM FOREST REGRESSOR]
- Purpose: Feature Importance Attribution.
- Goal: To identify which specific IPC/SLL sections are the primary "Drivers" of crime within a domain.
- Metric: Gini Importance / Mean Decrease in Impurity.
- Accuracy Results:
    * Women Domain: 84.90%
    * Juvenile Domain: 17.96%
- Key Finding: In "Women Crimes," Cruelty by Husband (Sec 498A) and Assault (Sec 354) were the dominant predictive features.

[MODEL D: LASSO LOGISTIC CLASSIFICATION (Risk Sentinel)]
- Purpose: Binary Threat Assessment.
- Goal: To classify a state as either "HIGH RISK" or "STABLE/LOW RISK."
- Classification Logic: Top 50% of states by intensity are marked as High Risk.
- Hyperparameters: L1 Regularization (Lasso) to eliminate noise features.
- Evaluation Metrics: Accuracy & Confusion Matrix.
- Accuracy Results: 
    * Women/Children Domains: 85% - 100% Accuracy.
    * Interpretation: Extremely reliable for early-warning flagging.

[MODEL E: REGRESSOR SUITE (Linear, Ridge, Lasso)]
- Purpose: Efficiency & Benchmarking.
- Goal: To determine if simple linear relationships explain the data better than complex ones.
- Evaluation Metrics: Mean Squared Error (MSE) & R-Squared.
- Finding: Linear and Ridge models showed very high R2 (0.90+), confirming that crime growth in India follows established linear trajectories.

[ENGINE X: CrimeExplainer (SHAP Intelligence Hub)]
- Purpose: Local Interpretability & Actionable Intelligence.
- Technology: SHAP (SHAPley Additive exPlanations).
- Logic: Automatically identifies the top 10 crime categories influencing a state's risk profile.
- Outputs:
    * Intelligence Briefings: Professional narratives explaining risk drivers.
    * Strategic Directives: Mapped interventions (e.g., 'Cruelty' -> Monitoring, 'Rape' -> Fast-Track Courts).

3. PREDICTION TARGETS (2026)
----------------------------
The models are trained to output two primary signals on the dashboard:
1. PREDICTED INTENSITY: The raw expected intensity score for 2026.
2. RISK GRADE: The binary label derived from the Sentinel Classifier.
